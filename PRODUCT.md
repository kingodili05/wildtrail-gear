# WildTrail Gear — Product Brief

## Register
**Brand.** Marketing-led luxury e-commerce. Design IS the product. Visitors decide whether they trust the operation in the first 4 seconds; design carries that load.

## Audience
- **Premium expedition operators** — film crews, hunting outfitters, off-grid researchers buying $4k–$10k systems on company cards. Read carefully, expect specificity.
- **High-net-worth outdoor enthusiasts** — wealthy customers who want gear that signals seriousness. Read fast, decide on feel.
- **Brand/press partners** — agencies, retailers, reviewers evaluating whether WildTrail is the real article or AI-generated SaaS slop.

## Product purpose
1. Sell seven hyper-premium SKUs at high average order value (target AOV $4,200).
2. Telegraph engineering-grade credibility on first scroll.
3. Host a continuous live operator broadcast that doubles as social proof.
4. Convert without ever feeling like a generic Shopify catalog.

## Tone / voice
Three concrete words: **engineered, deliberate, weather-worn**.
- Engineered: technical numbers, serialized SKUs, telemetry. Not vibes.
- Deliberate: not loud. Restraint sells $8,500 streaming rigs better than glitter.
- Weather-worn: real fieldwork — mud, dust, condensation — not Pinterest cleanliness.

## Anti-references
- **Generic Shopify dropshipper template.** Pastel hero, three-icon trust strip, "100% Satisfaction" badge.
- **YETI / Patagonia warm-cream brand register.** Not us. Warmer brands feel coastal; we are continental and tactical.
- **Crypto / Web3 dark glass holographic.** Neon gradients, glassmorphism, "Sign in with wallet" energy.
- **Marketplace listing site.** Stripe-purple, Instrument-Serif headers, three-column reviews. Saturated reflex.

## Direct references
- **Aesop** for restraint and typographic discipline.
- **Bang & Olufsen** for product-as-sculpture photography and quiet luxury.
- **Linear Engineering changelog** for crisp technical typography and precise micro-details.
- **Sitka Gear / KUIU** for tactical-outdoor color discipline and field photography.
- **Apple Pro Display XDR product page** for considered 3D product showcases.

## Strategic principles
- **Specificity sells.** Numbers, serials, telemetry. Generic words ("premium," "innovative") are forbidden.
- **3D used like spice, not sauce.** One ambient 3D scene per major surface. Never decorative-only.
- **Hover is for desktop; touch surfaces get their own dance.** Tilt, parallax, and zoom states only fire on `(hover: hover)`; touch gets stronger pressed-states.
- **No emoji.** Lucide SVG icons or nothing. Same rule as hannah-barron.
- **Conversion is implicit.** One ask per fold. Never compete CTAs.
